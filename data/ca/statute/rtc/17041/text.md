# California Revenue and Taxation Code Section 17041

Source: https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/codes_displaySection.xhtml?sectionNum=17041.&lawCode=RTC

## (a) Resident Tax Rates (Non-Head of Household)

(1) There shall be imposed for each taxable year upon the entire taxable income of every resident of this state, except the head of a household as defined in Section 17042, and except as provided in Part 10.2 (commencing with Section 18401) of Division 2, taxes in the following amounts and at the following rates upon the amount of taxable income computed for that year as if the resident were a resident of this state for the entire taxable year and for all prior taxable years for any carryover items, deferred income, suspended losses, or suspended deductions:

| If the taxable income is: | The tax is: |
|---------------------------|-------------|
| Not over $3,650 | 1% of the taxable income |
| Over $3,650 but not over $8,650 | $36.50 plus 2% of the excess over $3,650 |
| Over $8,650 but not over $13,650 | $136.50 plus 4% of the excess over $8,650 |
| Over $13,650 but not over $18,950 | $336.50 plus 6% of the excess over $13,650 |
| Over $18,950 but not over $23,950 | $654.50 plus 8% of the excess over $18,950 |
| Over $23,950 | $1,054.50 plus 9.3% of the excess over $23,950 |

(2) For each taxable year beginning on or after January 1, 2009, and before January 1, 2011, the percentage specified in the last row of the table in paragraph (1) shall be increased by 0.25 percent.

## (b) Nonresident and Part-Year Resident Tax (Non-Head of Household)

(1) There shall be imposed for each taxable year upon the taxable income of every nonresident or part-year resident of this state, except the head of a household as defined in Section 17042, taxes computed as specified in paragraph (2).

(2) The tax shall be the amount computed under subdivision (a), calculated as if the nonresident or part-year resident were a resident of this state for the entire taxable year and for all prior taxable years for any carryover items, deferred income, suspended losses, or suspended deductions, multiplied by the ratio of the taxable income of the nonresident or part-year resident, as defined in subdivision (i), computed without regard to any deductions for personal exemption, to the amount of taxable income, as computed for purposes of determining tax under this subdivision.

## (c) Head of Household Tax Rates

(1) There shall be imposed for each taxable year upon the entire taxable income of every resident of this state who is the head of a household as defined in Section 17042, and except as provided in Part 10.2 (commencing with Section 18401) of Division 2, taxes in the following amounts and at the following rates upon the amount of taxable income computed for that year as if the resident were a resident of this state for the entire taxable year and for all prior taxable years for any carryover items, deferred income, suspended losses, or suspended deductions:

| If the taxable income is: | The tax is: |
|---------------------------|-------------|
| Not over $7,300 | 1% of the taxable income |
| Over $7,300 but not over $17,300 | $73 plus 2% of the excess over $7,300 |
| Over $17,300 but not over $22,300 | $273 plus 4% of the excess over $17,300 |
| Over $22,300 but not over $27,600 | $473 plus 6% of the excess over $22,300 |
| Over $27,600 but not over $32,600 | $791 plus 8% of the excess over $27,600 |
| Over $32,600 | $1,191 plus 9.3% of the excess over $32,600 |

(2) For each taxable year beginning on or after January 1, 2009, and before January 1, 2011, the percentage specified in the last row of the table in paragraph (1) shall be increased by 0.25 percent.

## (d) Nonresident Head of Household Tax

(1) There shall be imposed for each taxable year upon the taxable income of every nonresident or part-year resident of this state who is the head of a household as defined in Section 17042, taxes computed as specified in paragraph (2).

(2) The tax shall be the amount computed under subdivision (c), calculated as if the nonresident or part-year resident were a resident of this state for the entire taxable year and for all prior taxable years for any carryover items, deferred income, suspended losses, or suspended deductions, multiplied by the ratio of the taxable income of the nonresident or part-year resident, as defined in subdivision (i), computed without regard to any deductions for personal exemption, to the amount of taxable income, as computed for purposes of determining tax under this subdivision.

## (e) Estates and Trusts

There shall be imposed for each taxable year on the taxable income of an estate or trust, an amount equal to the amount computed under subdivision (a) as if the estate or trust were an individual having the same amount of taxable income.

## (f) Tax Classification

The tax imposed by this section is not a surtax.

## (g) Unearned Income of Children

(1) Section 1(g) of the Internal Revenue Code, relating to certain unearned income of children taxed as if parent's income, shall apply, except as otherwise provided.

(2) Section 1(g)(7)(B)(ii)(II) of the Internal Revenue Code is modified by substituting "1 percent" for "10 percent."

## (h) Inflation Adjustment of Tax Brackets

(1) For each taxable year beginning on or after January 1, 1988, the California Department of Industrial Relations shall transmit to the Franchise Tax Board the percentage change in the California Consumer Price Index for all items from June of the prior calendar year to June of the current calendar year, no later than August 1 of the current calendar year.

(2) The Franchise Tax Board shall do both of the following:

(A) Compute an inflation adjustment factor by adding 100 percent to the percentage change figure that is furnished pursuant to paragraph (1) and dividing the result by 100.

(B) Multiply the preceding taxable year's income tax brackets by the inflation adjustment factor and round off the resulting amounts to the nearest one dollar ($1).

## (i) Taxable Income of Nonresident or Part-Year Resident

(1) "Taxable income of a nonresident or part-year resident," for purposes of computing tax under this section, includes each of the following:

(A) For any part of the taxable year during which the taxpayer was a resident of this state, all items of gross income and all deductions, regardless of source.

(B) For any part of the taxable year during which the taxpayer was not a resident of this state, gross income and deductions derived from sources within this state, determined in accordance with Article 17 (commencing with Section 17951) of Chapter 12.

(2) If a net operating loss is sustained during the taxable year by a nonresident or part-year resident, the net operating loss shall be computed, for purposes of this section, only to the extent attributable to the part of the taxable year during which the taxpayer was a resident of this state, plus the net operating loss attributable to income and deductions derived from sources within this state during the part of the taxable year during which the taxpayer was not a resident.

(3) For purposes of this section, any carryover items, deferred income, suspended losses, or suspended deductions shall be includable or allowable only if the carryover items, deferred income, suspended losses, or suspended deductions were derived from sources within this state.
