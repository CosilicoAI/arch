# 26 USC ยง 32 - Earned Income

## Summary

The Earned Income Tax Credit (EITC) is a refundable tax credit for low-to-moderate income workers.

## (a) Allowance of Credit

In the case of an eligible individual, there shall be allowed as a credit against the tax imposed
an amount equal to the credit percentage of so much of the taxpayer's earned income for the
taxable year as does not exceed the earned income amount.

### (a)(2) Limitation

The amount of the credit shall not exceed the excess (if any) of:
- (A) the credit percentage of the earned income amount, over
- (B) the phaseout percentage of so much of the adjusted gross income (or, if greater, the
  earned income) of the taxpayer for the taxable year as exceeds the phaseout amount.

## (b) Percentages and Amounts

### (b)(1) Percentages

| Qualifying Children | Credit Percentage | Phaseout Percentage |
|---------------------|-------------------|---------------------|
| 1 child             | 34%               | 15.98%              |
| 2 children          | 40%               | 21.06%              |
| 3 or more children  | 45%               | 21.06%              |
| No children         | 7.65%             | 7.65%               |

### (b)(2) Amounts (Base Year 2015)

| Qualifying Children | Earned Income Amount | Phaseout Amount |
|---------------------|---------------------|-----------------|
| 1 child             | $6,330              | $11,610         |
| 2 or more children  | $8,890              | $11,610         |
| No children         | $4,220              | $5,280          |

### (b)(2)(B) Joint Returns

In the case of a joint return filed by an eligible individual and such individual's spouse,
the phaseout amount shall be increased by $5,000.

### (b)(3) Inflation Adjustments

Dollar amounts are adjusted annually for inflation using the Chained CPI-U, with 2015 as base year.

## (c) Definitions

### (c)(1) Eligible Individual

#### (c)(1)(A) With Qualifying Child
An individual who has a qualifying child for the taxable year.

#### (c)(1)(A)(ii) Without Qualifying Child
An individual who:
- (I) has a principal place of abode in the United States for more than one-half of the year
- (II) has attained age 25 but not attained age 65 before the close of the taxable year
- (III) is not a dependent for whom a deduction is allowable under section 151 to another taxpayer

### (c)(2) Earned Income

The term "earned income" means:
- (A) wages, salaries, tips, and other employee compensation, but only if includible in gross income
- (B) the amount of the taxpayer's net earnings from self-employment

## (i) Denial of Credit for Individuals Having Excessive Investment Income

### (i)(1) In General

No credit shall be allowed under subsection (a) for the taxable year if the aggregate amount
of disqualified income of the taxpayer for the taxable year exceeds $10,000 (adjusted for inflation).

### (i)(2) Disqualified Income

The term "disqualified income" includes:
- (A) interest
- (B) dividends
- (C) net rental income
- (D) capital gain net income
- (E) net passive activity income
